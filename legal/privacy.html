How we collect, use, and safeguard information on CloseDose.com.

Effective Date: October 02, 2025

SCOPE

This Privacy Policy explains how CloseDose LLC collects, uses, and shares information when you use CloseDose.com and related services (collectively, the “Services”).

WHAT WE COLLECT

We aim to avoid collecting personal data. We do not request names, birth dates, or health records. We may collect limited device and usage data—such as IP address, browser type, pages viewed, referring URLs, and timestamps—through first-party analytics or privacy-respecting tools. If you contact us, we will receive the information you provide (for example, your email address and message content).

COOKIES

We may use strictly necessary cookies to maintain session preferences and optional analytics cookies to understand feature usage. You can control cookies through your browser settings.

USE OF INFORMATION

We use information to operate, maintain, and improve the Services; detect, prevent, and address technical issues; and respond to inquiries.

SHARING

We do not sell personal information. We may share limited data with service providers who help us operate the Services (for example, hosting and analytics) under confidentiality obligations, or when required by law.

PHI

We do not request or store Protected Health Information (PHI). Do not enter identifiable health information into free-text forms.

DATA SECURITY & RETENTION

We use reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect the limited data we handle. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure. We retain data only as long as necessary for the purposes described in this Policy.

CHILDREN’S PRIVACY

The Services are not directed to children under 13. Parents and caregivers may use the dosing calculators for their dependents.

YOUR CHOICES

You may disable cookies, use browser privacy controls, or contact us to request deletion of messages you have sent to us.

INTERNATIONAL USERS

If you access the Services from outside the United States, you consent to processing in the United States and to U.S. law.
